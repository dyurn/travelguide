Marine Mammals

Monterey Bay is home to a variety of marine mammals, such as sea otters, seals, sea lions, whales, and dolphins. These animals can be found throughout the bay, with many species frequently seen near the shoreline and in the kelp forests.

Fish

With its nutrient-rich waters, Monterey Bay supports a diverse array of fish species. Some of the most common fish found in the bay include rockfish, flatfish, and various species of shark. The bay also serves as a critical habitat for several endangered fish species.

Seabirds

The coastal environment and rich marine life of Monterey Bay make it an ideal habitat for many species of seabirds. Cormorants, pelicans, and various species of gulls and terns can be found nesting along the shoreline and feeding in the bay's waters.

Marine Invertebrates

Monterey Bay's diverse underwater habitats support a wide variety of marine invertebrates, including sea stars, urchins, anemones, crabs, and octopuses. The bay's kelp forests and rocky reefs are particularly important for supporting these species.
