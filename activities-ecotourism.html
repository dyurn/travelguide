Whale Watching

Monterey Bay is one of the best places in the world for whale watching. Throughout the year, various species of whales can be seen in the bay, including humpback, gray, and blue whales. There are numerous whale watching tour operators in the area, and many practice responsible and sustainable tourism.

Kayaking

Kayaking is a popular activity in Monterey Bay, allowing visitors to explore the bay's waters and get up close to marine life such as sea otters, seals, and seabirds. Many local outfitters offer kayak rentals and guided tours, with a focus on ecotourism and minimal disturbance to the wildlife.

Scuba Diving & Snorkeling

Monterey Bay's diverse underwater habitats make it an excellent destination for scuba diving and snorkeling. The bay's kelp forests, rocky reefs, and sandy seafloors are home to a wide variety of marine life. Local dive shops and tour operators offer equipment rentals and guided dives, emphasizing responsible and sustainable practices.

Wildlife Education & Interpretive Programs

Many organizations and facilities around Monterey Bay offer educational programs and interpretive walks, aimed at teaching visitors about the bay's unique ecosystem and the importance of conservation. Some popular options include the Monterey Bay Aquarium, the Pacific Grove Museum of Natural History, and the Elkhorn Slough National Estuarine Research Reserve.
